Our story

We help Indian women get ahead of health problems their standard blood tests would never catch.

Built by women, for women. Starting from our own experience.

Why we built this

We started BioWellth out of frustration. Too many women in India, including people close to us, were being told their results were normal while their health was quietly getting worse. The markers that would have explained it were never tested. The connections were never made.

We knew what comprehensive testing could reveal, because we'd seen it ourselves. And we knew the current system wasn't going to change on its own. So we built the platform we wished had existed.

The pattern

Ferritin at 28. Haemoglobin borderline. Symptoms present for years. Every test declared normal. The markers that would have explained it were simply never run.

The early warning

No symptoms. Feeling well. But biomarkers quietly drifting in the wrong direction. The kind of drift that standard tests miss until it becomes a diagnosis.

How we think

Three principles

Test what matters

Standard panels are designed to catch disease, not support wellbeing. We built a female-specific panel that includes the markers most commonly missed: ferritin, free thyroid hormones, sex hormones, and more.

Read results honestly

Population-based reference ranges tell you whether you're in the worst 5%. Functional ranges tell you whether you're well. We use the latter, and explain what the difference actually means for you.

Track what changes

A single test is a photograph. Longitudinal tracking is a film. Patterns visible across months and years, in biomarkers, symptoms, and cycle, reveal what snapshots never can.

Our mission

To make comprehensive, functional health intelligence accessible to every Indian woman so she can understand her own biology, catch what's drifting, and stop being told she's fine when she isn't.

Optimal ranges, not just clinical thresholds

Clinical reference ranges are set to identify disease in a broad population. They won't tell you whether you're actually thriving. BioWellth reads your results against functional optimal ranges, explains what each marker actually means, and connects them to your symptoms and cycle.

  • Functional ranges, not just "normal / abnormal"
  • Marker-by-marker plain-language explanation
  • Connections to your symptoms, energy, and cycle
  • What to watch, what to act on, what to ask your doctor
Your ferritin is 28 ng/mL. Standard ranges would consider this borderline, but BioWellth flags it as functionally low for your activity level and cycle pattern. This commonly explains fatigue, hair shedding, and poor sleep quality.
What should I do about it?
Three things: increase dietary iron with a vitamin C source at the same meal, ask your doctor to investigate the underlying cause (your last scan noted something worth revisiting), and retest in 12 weeks on your next panel.
