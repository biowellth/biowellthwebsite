Our story

We help Indian women get ahead of health problems their standard blood tests would never catch.

Built by women, for women. Starting from our own experience.

Why we built this

We started BioWellth out of frustration. Women close to us were being told their results were normal while their health was getting worse. The markers that would have explained it were never run.

We'd seen what a full panel could reveal. The system wasn't going to change on its own. So we built the platform we wished had existed.

The pattern

Ferritin at 28. Haemoglobin borderline. Symptoms present for years. Every test declared normal. The markers that would have explained it were simply never run.

The early warning

No symptoms. Feeling well. But markers quietly moving the wrong way, the kind of change standard tests miss until it's already a diagnosis.

How we think

Three principles

Test what matters

Standard panels are built to catch disease. We built one that tells you whether you're actually well. That means including the markers most checkups skip, like ferritin, free thyroid hormones, and sex hormones.

Read results honestly

Reference ranges tell you whether you're in the worst 5%. Functional ranges tell you whether you're well. We use the latter.

Track what changes

One annual test misses everything in between. We track markers, symptoms, and cycle together so the gradual changes don't get lost.

Our mission

Our goal is simple. Every woman in India should be able to actually see how she's doing, not just hear that she's fine.

Why am I so exhausted in the second half of my cycle every month?
Looking at your data, your progesterone tends to dip in the luteal phase, your cortisol rises around day 18, and your sleep quality drops noticeably in that same window. Those things can feed into each other, so it makes sense that the second half of your cycle feels heavier.
What should I be eating?
Foods high in B6 and zinc can support progesterone production, so things like eggs, chickpeas, and pumpkin seeds are worth adding in. Alcohol and refined sugar tend to push cortisol up, so they're worth pulling back on in the luteal phase. A magnesium supplement at night might also help with sleep quality in that window.
